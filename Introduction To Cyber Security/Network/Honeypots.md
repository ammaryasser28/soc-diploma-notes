A **Honeypot** is a deliberately vulnerable system placed within a network to attract attackers.  
It is designed to look like a legitimate and exploitable target, while its real purpose is to **detect, monitor, and analyze malicious activities**.

Unlike production systems, a honeypot does **not serve any real users or business functions**.

---

## ðŸŽ¯ Purpose of a Honeypot

- Attract attackers away from critical systems and assets
- Detect unauthorized access attempts
- Monitor attacker behavior and techniques
- Improve overall network security awareness
- Act as an early warning system for potential attacks

---

## ðŸ” Key Characteristics

- **Intentionally Vulnerable**  
  The system is configured with known weaknesses to make it appealing to attackers.

- **No Legitimate User Access**  
  There is no valid reason for normal users to access a honeypot. Any interaction is considered suspicious.

- **Requires Illegal Discovery to Access**  
  Accessing the honeypot typically requires activities such as network scanning or discovery, which are unauthorized and prohibited.

- **Imitates Real Systems**  
  The honeypot is designed to resemble real servers, services, or applications to appear authentic.

- **Fully Monitored**  
  All actions performed by attackers are logged and analyzed for security research and defense improvement.
